Technology Overview

The AI chip within our artificial heart is engineered to detect abnormalities in heart rhythms. The device is equipped with sensors that continuously monitor the heart's electrical activity, allowing for real-time adjustments to heart function, thereby ensuring that the heart maintains an optimal rhythm even in the presence of arrhythmias.

How It Works

The AI-powered artificial heart continuously monitors the patient’s heart activity and adjusts in real-time to prevent cardiac events. The heart works by contracting and relaxing to pump blood throughout the body. This process is controlled by electrical impulses that trigger heartbeats. When these impulses are disrupted, it can lead to arrhythmias or even heart failure. Our device can potentially delay heart attacks by:

  • **Detecting early warning signs of irregularities:** The AI chip will analyze data from the heart's electrical activity to identify patterns that indicate potential problems.
  • **Providing feedback to the patient and healthcare providers:** Real-time data transmission to medical professionals ensures that any anomalies are addressed promptly.
  • **Adjusting medication dosages based on heart rhythms:** The device can communicate with healthcare systems to automatically adjust medications as needed, based on the patient's heart activity.

Mechanism of the Heart

The human heart is a complex organ that functions as a pump to circulate blood throughout the body. It consists of four chambers: the right and left atria, and the right and left ventricles. Blood flows from the body into the right atrium, moves to the right ventricle, and is then pumped to the lungs for oxygenation. Oxygen-rich blood returns to the left atrium, flows into the left ventricle, and is pumped out to the rest of the body. This intricate process is regulated by a network of electrical impulses generated by the sinoatrial (SA) node, which serves as the natural pacemaker of the heart.

Electrical signals travel through specialized pathways in the heart, causing the muscles to contract in a coordinated manner. Any disruptions in this electrical system can lead to conditions such as atrial fibrillation, ventricular tachycardia, or complete heart block, all of which can increase the risk of heart attack and other serious complications.
